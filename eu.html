  • What are the key reasons of Deforestation in Europe?

    Key drivers/reasons of deforestation in Europe include Agricultural Expansion, Over-exploitation of wood resources, Urbanization and infrastructure development and Climate change.

  • How does deforestaion in Europe affect the environment?

    Deforestation in Europe has significant environmental consequences, including being a main driver of climate change and biodiversity loss. Forests play a crucial role in absorbing CO2, regulating the water cycle, and providing habitats for biodiversity. When forests are cleared, the stored carbon is released into the atmosphere, contributing to greenhouse gas emissions and global warming. Additionally, deforestation leads to the loss of habitats for countless plant and animal species, threatening their survival and reducing overall biodiversity. The European Union has recognized the impact of its consumption on deforestation and has taken steps to address this issue, such as implementing regulations to limit the importation of goods associated with deforestation. Efforts to combat deforestation in Europe reflect the EU's commitment to addressing this environmental challenge and promoting sustainable forest management practices

  • Has any action been taken against this growing issue?

    Many laws and policies have been established to tackle deforestation. They include:- EU Timber Regulation (EUTR), FLEGT (Forest Law Enforcement, Governance, and Trade), New EU Legislation on Deforestation, EU Consumption and Deforestation, European Green Deal.
